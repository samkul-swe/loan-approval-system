## How She Works

Sampada's strongest quality is her ability to reason through problems before touching code. She consistently questioned assumptions, pushed back on answers that didn't feel right, and refused to accept explanations that didn't make intuitive sense to her. She drove the direction of every decision — Claude provided context, corrected mistakes and filled gaps, but the thinking was hers.

She used Claude as a thinking partner rather than a code generator. She rarely asked for code without first understanding what that code should do and why. ## What She Knows Well

**Business thinking applied to ML** — she independently identified cost asymmetry, cold start risk, trial loan budgeting, and the difference between a data problem and a model problem. These are things senior ML engineers are expected to know but many don't think about naturally.

**Reasoning from first principles** — she arrived at XGBoost, the need for calibration, confident learning, and tier-based decisions through her own logic. She didn't memorise answers — she derived them.

**Feature intuition** — she understood which features were leaky, which were correlated, and which needed engineering before any model was trained. Her instinct on credit_utilization_pressure as a meaningful signal was confirmed by the data.

**Product and fairness awareness** — she flagged redlining risk with geographic features, understood the implications of proxy variables, and recognised that model decisions have legal and ethical consequences.

## What She Is Still Developing

**Statistical depth** — calibration, disparate impact testing, proper holdout methodology, hypothesis testing. She has intuition for these concepts but not yet formal fluency.

**Precision in problem definition** — she occasionally conflates two related problems when one feels similar to the other. Developing the habit of precisely defining a problem before proposing a solution would sharpen her thinking.

**Production ML experience** — she has designed production concepts (drift detection, canary deployment, feedback loops) but has not yet built and operated them at scale with real traffic and real consequences.

**Tendency to lead with intuition** — her instincts are often right, but building the habit of validating them against data before acting on them would make her more rigorous.
